PEJA

Surrounded by the dramatic peaks of the Rugova Mountains, Peja is a city where nature and history exist side by side. Known as the gateway to the Rugova Gorge, Peja offers breathtaking landscapes, fresh mountain air, and a deep sense of tradition.

With its strong cultural identity, spiritual heritage, and close connection to nature, Peja represents one of Kosovo’s most unique and authentic cities.

The Story of Peja

Peja is one of the oldest cities in Kosovo, with a history that dates back to ancient Illyrian and Roman times. Its strategic position near mountain passes made it an important center for trade, culture, and religion throughout the centuries.

During the medieval period, Peja became a significant spiritual and administrative center. It later flourished under Ottoman rule, developing traditional crafts such as metalwork, wood carving, and textile production, many of which are still practiced today.

Today, Peja is a vibrant city that balances modern life with tradition. It is known for its hospitality, strong community values, and its role as a starting point for exploring some of Kosovo’s most stunning natural landscapes.

Landmarks & Highlights

Patriarchate of Peja

The Patriarchate of Peja is one of the most important medieval religious monuments in the region. Dating back to the 13th century, it served as the spiritual seat of the Serbian Orthodox Church and is recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage site.

Rugova Gorge

Just outside the city lies the spectacular Rugova Gorge, a natural wonder carved by the Lumbardhi River. With towering cliffs, hiking trails, and mountain villages, it is one of Kosovo’s most visited and admired natural attractions.

Bajrakli Mosque

Built during the Ottoman period, the Bajrakli Mosque is one of Peja’s oldest religious landmarks. It reflects the city’s Islamic heritage and remains an important place of worship and community gathering.

Old Bazaar (Çarshia e Pejës)

The Old Bazaar of Peja is a lively area filled with traditional shops, cafés, and artisan workshops. It represents the economic and social heart of the city, preserving the spirit of Peja’s historic trade routes.
